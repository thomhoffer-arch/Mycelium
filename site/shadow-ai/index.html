Someone on your team has already pasted a tender price, a defect report, or a contract clause into a free cloud chatbot to "just check something". 1. What shadow AI is — and why AEC is exposed.

"Shadow AI" is the use of AI tools without the knowledge, approval or oversight of the people accountable for the project: free-tier chatbots, browser plug-ins, embedded SaaS features, ungoverned agents. Industry surveys in 2025 put adoption past 80% of employees, while only around a third of organisations have any AI governance in place — and IBM's 2025 Cost of a Data Breach report tied roughly one in five breaches at affected organisations to unsanctioned AI.

AEC is more exposed than most. The data getting pasted out isn't a marketing draft — it's priced bills of quantities, defect-liability evidence, named personal data, and contract terms, on a project that stays legally live for ten years or more across a dozen firms. Once it leaves for someone else's model, you can't recall it, and you can't prove what it was used to decide.

2. The black-box cloud copilot is the problem, not the cure.

The standard answer on the market today is a hosted copilot: ship your project into a vendor's cloud, let an opaque model rank it, and trust the result. That pattern is shadow AI with a logo on it. The data still flows outward to an external model, and when a client, an insurer or an arbitrator asks "which AI suggested this, who approved it, and when?", there is no answer — the reasoning, the inputs and the approval all happened somewhere you can't reach.

Banning it doesn't work either: when AI is prohibited, roughly half of people keep using personal accounts anyway. The only thing that actually moves the needle is a governed alternative that's better than the shadow one — same speed, with the receipts.

3. Mycelium Studio: governed AI, by construction.

Mycelium Studio is the intelligence layer that grows on the open Connective Spine. Every property that makes shadow AI dangerous is designed out of it:

Your data never leaves to be ranked.

The intelligence runs inside your environment — your laptop, your server, your VPC — against the connectors you already trust. There is no mandatory upload and no shared bucket. The model comes to the data, not the other way around.

The agent proposes; a human disposes.

Mycelium Studio never executes a change on its own. It emits a proposal that a named human approves before anything is written. Actors are recorded as pseudonymous refs — agent:llm, human:u_8f3a — so the audit trail captures the decision without leaking the person (and survives a GDPR erasure request intact).

Every AI decision is on a tamper-evident ledger.

Each proposal, approval and executed change is appended to a log, and every entry carries a SHA-256 hash of the one before it. Rewrite a single event and every later hash stops matching. "Which AI suggested this, who signed off, and when?" stops being unanswerable and becomes one file you can hand over — to a court, an insurer, or an EU AI Act auditor.

No black box, no lock-in.

The spine it runs on is open (Apache-2.0) and the contract is public, so the intelligence layer is interchangeable: self-host it, swap it, or audit it. You are never trapped inside a vendor whose model you can't see and can't leave.

From shadow AI to accountable AI.

The goal isn't to ban AI from the jobsite — that battle is already lost. It's to make the AI that's already there answerable: data that stays yours, decisions a human owns, and a record that holds up when someone asks who decided what. That's the difference between a copilot you have to trust and one you can prove.
