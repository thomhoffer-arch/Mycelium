An AI orchestrator for AEC, where the data lives.

Mycelium is the open spine: the contract that lets model, clash, finance and field data join across tools. The intelligence layer is what runs on top of that spine — an AI orchestrator that joins the records, ranks what matters, and writes back only under human approval. Sovereign by construction. Cited by default. Accountable in a ledger.

What an orchestrator does

An AEC AI agent is only useful if it can pull together what's currently in five tools — the Revit model, the clash detector, the BCF issues, the ERP purchase orders, the site reports — and answer one question with one provenance. The orchestrator is the program that does that joining: it consumes spine records from any connector, follows the identity edges (uniqueId, ifcGuid, classification, work-package, zone), and triages by whatever your project's profile says matters.

It is not a chatbot, and it is not a BIM viewer. It is the layer that turns "what does the model say × what does finance say × what did the inspector see" into one decision-grade answer.

Sovereign by construction

The orchestrator runs where your data already lives — your laptop, your server, your VPC. There is no shared cloud you have to upload your project to. Connectors fetch from the source they already have access to; the LLM call can be a local model or a hosted one your firm has approved. No US SaaS dependency in the base layer.

This matters for EU firms working under GDPR, for public-sector projects with sovereignty clauses, and for any office that doesn't want to send drawings to a black box.

Cited and accountable — the AI audit trail

Every join, proposal and approved write is appended to the same tamper-evident provenance ledger the spine defines. Actors are pseudonymous refs (agent:llm, human:u_8f3a) — never raw names or emails. Six months later you can prove exactly which model revision, which clash, which approver, which prompt.

Hand the file to an insurer or a court. The chain verifies; no platform between you and the evidence.

Open base, private intelligence

Mycelium is open (Apache-2.0). Connectors are open. The contract is forkable. The orchestrator on top is where the accumulated judgement — triage rules, project profiles, ranking — lives, and that is not part of the open standard. Different orchestrators can compete on top of the same spine. No single vendor captures the network.
